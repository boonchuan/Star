Chapter 1: The First Divergence

The night it began, the skies were clear—until the stars fell. A fragment from the Big Dipper, luminous and wrong, tore through the stratosphere and crashed into Earth. The impact zone: a forgotten mountain observatory where time and space warped. Scientists sent to investigate returned altered, incoherent, or dead.

One man, later known as Patient Zero, touched the shard. It fused with his cells, merging cosmic radiation with a dormant prion-like strain of COVID-BSE. He didn't just carry infection—he carried purpose.

The first symptoms were innocuous: vivid dreams, ritualistic pacing, muttering phrases in dead languages. Then came the transformations. Communities collapsed. Cities quarantined. The Starfall Plague had begun.
